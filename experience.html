My Journey in Finance & Data

My interest in finance and data science has started with my dad being a computer scientist who continued to work at banks and financial service companies. Then as I went through high school and was able to take AP computer science classes I realized, like my dad, I liked problem solving and thinking analytically. Therefore, seeing him work at banks and after taking business classes, once I got to college, I realized finance was the career I wanted to go into.

My first internship was at a real estate firm, where I gained hands-on experience analyzing property investments and financial performance. During the internship, I assisted in developing financial models used to evaluate acquisition, development, and investment opportunities. This included building amortization schedules for mortgages and loan payments to better understand financing structures. I also tracked and updated performance metrics for more than 25 properties, analyzing key indicators such as cash flow, net operating income, and return on investment. In addition, I supported forecasting, budgeting, and valuation work for both existing properties and potential acquisitions

On the academic side, my coursework in machine learning and database systems has pushed me to think computationally about problems I encounter in finance. I've built classification models, designed relational databases, and created data visualizations that tell stories with numbers. I'm always looking for ways to bridge the gap between technical tools and business outcomes.

During my time at MFS Investment Management, I gained exposure to how institutional asset managers evaluate markets and communicate investment insights to clients. I worked with platforms such as Bloomberg, FactSet, and eVestment to analyze investment strategies and support client-facing materials. At MFS, I was also able to conduct over 50 coffee chats with many analysts in the company to learn about their role and advice they have for someone just starting their career in finance. This gave me valuable knowledge on how to do well in the financial service industry.

In the classroom, I’ve focused on building strong analytical skills through projects involving machine learning, data visualization, and database design. I have also tried to enhance my finance skill through stock pitches and stock tracking simulators.
