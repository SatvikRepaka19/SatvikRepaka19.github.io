About Me

Hi, I'm Satvik Repaka. I am a Data Science and Finance student at Northeastern University. I have a very strong interest in problem solving through data science and analytical thinking when it comes to business problems. I grew up in Warren, New Jersey, where I developed an early interest in both numbers and creativity, whether that was mastering a Rubik's Cube or analyzing sports statistics.

I was just on Co-op during the fall semester and I had an amazing, hands on experience during my time on Co-op. I worked as an Investment Solutions Co-op at MFS Investment Management in Boston, where I conducted market research using Bloomberg and FactSet terminals, built client-facing materials on equity and sustainability topics, and automated reporting tools like the MFS Market Monitor for weekly market updates. I also analyzed active manager performance using eVestment data, examining metrics like information ratio, tracking error, and active share across global equity strategies.

Outside of my professional work, I am the VP of Finance for Phi Delta Theta at Northeastern, managing over $50,000 in organizational funds for a 90+ member chapter. I introduced a new payment platform called Greekbill and made financial incentive structures so members would be more inclined to pay.

I originally started college as a Computer Science major, but switched to Data Science because I realized my true passion was finance. I enjoyed the problem solving and analytical thinking of data, but just coding was not my true passion. That shift really helped me dive into finance, learn more about the markets, and use my data science skills to enhance my technical skills in finance. Outside of academics and work, I love playing tennis, hanging out with friends DJing, dancing, and doing flips.

I currently have an internship opportunity for summer 2026 at BNY Mellon. At BNY, I will be an Asset Servicing Intern dealing with middle market activity and doing fund services such as calculating NAV.
