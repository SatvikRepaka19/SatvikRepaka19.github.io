About Me

Hi, I'm Satvik Repaka — a Data Science and Finance student at Northeastern University with a passion for using analytical thinking to solve real-world business problems. I grew up in Warren, New Jersey, where I developed an early interest in both numbers and creativity, whether that was mastering a Rubik's Cube or analyzing sports statistics.

Currently, I work as an Investment Solutions Co-op at MFS Investment Management in Boston, where I conduct market research using Bloomberg and FactSet terminals, build client-facing materials on equity and sustainability topics, and automate reporting tools like the MFS Market Monitor for weekly market updates. I also analyze active manager performance using eVestment data, examining metrics like information ratio, tracking error, and active share across global equity strategies.

Outside of my professional work, I serve as VP of Finance for Phi Delta Theta at Northeastern, managing over $50,000 in organizational funds for a 90+ member chapter. I introduced new payment platforms and financial incentive structures that meaningfully improved chapter operations.

I originally started college as a Computer Science major, but switched to Data Science because I realized my true passion was the business application of analytical thinking — not just writing code for its own sake. That shift has defined everything I've pursued since: finding the intersection of data, finance, and real-world impact. Outside of academics and work, I love playing tennis, DJing, dancing, and yes — doing backflips.

I'm currently exploring summer 2026 opportunities in finance operations and capital markets, where I can bring both quantitative skills and strong communication to the table. Feel free to explore my site and reach out!
